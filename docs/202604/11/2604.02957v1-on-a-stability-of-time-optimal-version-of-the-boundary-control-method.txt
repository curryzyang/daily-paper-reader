Abstract

# 0 Introduction 

‚ÄĘ A principal feature and advantage of the boundary control method (BCm, [1]) is a time-optimality of the procedure that determines manifolds and pa-rameters from the time-domain boundary inverse data (observations). The optimality means that for determination in the T -neighborhood of the bound-ary, the observations R2T on the time segment [0 , 2T ] are sufficient, while the observations on [0 , 2T ‚Ä≤] with T ‚Ä≤ < T are insufficient and the observations on [0 , 2T ‚Ä≤] with T ‚Ä≤ > T are redundant. This feature of the time-optimal recon-struction (TOR) reflects the fundamental property of the wave motion, which is the finiteness of the wave propagation velocity. Meanwhile, there are other versions of the BCm that use R2T ‚Ä≤

, T ‚Ä≤ > T : see, e.g., [4, 26, 28, 29, 31, 32]. 

‚ÄĘ The stability of BCm has been and continues to be studied in a series of papers by [16, 22, 27, 19, 32]. However, to our knowledge, all known quanti-tative results (estimates) are obtained for the versions, which work with the data [0 , 2T ‚Ä≤] with T ‚Ä≤ > T . Moreover, some experts doubt the existence of similar estimates for TOR [27]. Meanwhile, the physically natural character of TOR and examples of its successful numerical implementation [7, 8, 21, 23] inspire hope for the existence of at least a qualitative stability. The latter is the subject of our paper. Let ő© be a manifold with the boundary őď, ő© T ‚äā ő© the T -neighborhood of őď. We show that a convergence 

R2Tj ‚Üí R2T at őď √ó [0 , 2T ] leads to a convergence V Tj ‚Üí V T of the operators, which visualize invisible waves supported in ő© T . The latter convergence is equivalent to a convergence W Tj ‚Üí W T of the operators which create waves in ő© T . So, if ő© is known , which is the case in the given paper, R2Tj ‚Üí R2T

leads to W Tj ‚Üí W T . This is our main result. Its application, in which the wave evolution is governed by the equation utt ‚ąí ‚ąÜu + qu = 0, is con-sidered and, eventually, we arrive at convergence of the potentials qj ‚Üí q

in H‚ąí2(ő© T ). However, the important, difficult and challenging question on the quantitative estimates (the rate of convergence) in TOR remains open. Interesting results in this direction are recently obtained in [20]. 

‚ÄĘ The plan of the paper is as follows. Section 1 is a portion of operator theory concerning triangular factoriza-tion (TF) of positive operators. The concept of convergence regular on a nest of subspaces is introduced. A known result (Theorem 1 from [15]) is presented in a form convenient for subsequent application. Section 2 contains a brief description of the TOR for a dynamical system 2with boundary control governed by the wave equation utt ‚ąí ‚ąÜu + qu = 0. In Section 3 we show that Theorem 1 is applicable to TOR and provides a result on the weak operator convergence W Tj ‚Üí W T . Its consequence is some convergence qj ‚Üí q.

‚ÄĘ In the paper, we deal with a (small enough) times T < T 0 such that the subdomain ő© T0 is covered by the regular semigeodesic coordinates with the base on őď. This is done only for technical simplicity: some TOR operators turn out to be unitary (and not just isometric, as in the general case) and so on. All results in the paper are valid for arbitrary T < T ‚ąó, where T‚ąó := inf {T > 0 | ő©T = ő© } ‚©Ĺ ‚ąě is the filling time.

## 3.3 Comments 

‚ÄĘ According to traditional views in the IP-community, an inverse problem is considered to be completely solved if 

1) Uniqueness is established, i.e., the injectivity of the correspondence ‚ÄĚob-jects to be determined 7 ‚Üí inverse data‚ÄĚ is proven; 

2) A procedure that recovers the objects is developed; 

3) A stability of the determination procedure is established; 

4) Necessary and sufficient conditions for solvability of the problem ( inverse data characterization ) are found. 13 For time-domain problems, the BC-method provides the TOR procedure that settles 1) and 2) [3, 6]. It also offers some insight into 4) [14]. Our paper gives at least some answer to the question 3) . However, to claim that the time-optimal reconstruction problem is completely solved would be overstatement. We guess and hope that these results can be substantially improved. First of all, this concerns the quantitative stability estimates for TOR that is very important, difficult and challenging problem. 

‚ÄĘ The original version of TOR has appeared in [2]. In the same paper, repre-sentation (8) based on the A.S.Blagoveshchenskii relations, and the concept of the amplitude integral (later interpreted as an operator diagonal) have been proposed, It generalizes M,S.Brodskii‚Äôs operator construction known as triangular truncation integral [24]. The approach [2], with no substan-tial modifications, is generalized in [11] for the reconstruction of Riemannian manifolds. In [10], a variant of TOR uses visualization of Gaussian beams for reconstruction.
